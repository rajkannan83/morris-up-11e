  1. Which of the following questions would most likely be of greatest interest to a developmental psychologist?

    1. correlational research.

    2. naturalistic observation.

    3. a controlled experiment.

    2. They are more likely to use a biased sample.

  13. Consider the following experiment. Children are drawn from high-, middle-, and low-income households. All the children are divided into two groups. One group watches “Sesame Street” on TV every day for a month. The other group watches cartoons. Both groups are then given a test that measures creativity. In this study, the independent variable is:

    1. There is no independent variable; this is a correlational study.

    2. the children’s socioeconomic class.

    3. the children’s scores on the creativity test.

    4. what the children watch on TV.
